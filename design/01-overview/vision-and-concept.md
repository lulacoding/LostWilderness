# Vision and Concept

Design vision and core idea for Lost Wilderness.

---

## Core Idea

Lost Wilderness is a **biblically-inspired “Earth simulator”** Minecraft server. The naming is intentional: “Lost in the Wilderness” maps to the Lord, the Redeemer, and El Diablo—**not** direct stand-ins for God, Jesus, and Satan, but a server-world mythology that drives the storyline and theming.

From the chats:

- The server is described as **highly customised vanilla, no-hacks anarchy**, supporting **both Java and Bedrock** with a goal of being a **Featured Server on the Bedrock list**.
- It is **free-to-play (F2P)** with a **small one-off VIP** option.
- The design aim is to turn the game into something like **“Elden Ring”** in terms of progression—taking a casual sandbox and making it a **real-time strategy MMORPG purist anarchy** experience.
- The team explicitly decided **not** to add heavy mods so that **strong anticheat can work** and **Bedrock players get a full experience**; the tagline “**only SMP with Amplified**” on Bedrock is a selling point.
- There is a described **“large void in the market”** for highly customised vanilla no-hacks anarchy on **1.21.6**, which Lost Wilderness is targeting.

---

## Story Premise

The overarching story is to **restore multiversal cosmic peace** and **defeat El Diablo** to remove the “Problem of Evil” from the earthly realms. That narrative drives the 7-chapter storyline, post-game Galactic Journey (Angel → Arch Angel), and the gates behind which content sits (El Diablo, Ender Dragon, Father of Ender, 64 Ultimate personalities, 5 elements mastered).

---

## Server Name and Feel

The name **“Lost Wilderness”** was described as increasingly fitting as development went on. The **Equator zone** is intended as a buffer for new players and to encourage exploration. **Nomadic playstyle** is supported—players who do not join a clan and instead “follow seasons” or move around are valid; the server supports a “wandering trader” style of play.

---

## Calendar and Seasons in the Vision

The **calendar plugin** is not just a utility: it is the **internal plugin clock** for the server. Eclipses are tied to it (every **90–98 days** in discussion). **Seasonal gameplay** is part of the vision: e.g. farming in spring, mining/building in winter, with the calendar tracking days, months, and years so that in-game history (wars, events, “museum” dates) can be recorded and referenced. One message described the last server reaching the **4th century** after about three years, with “quite a bit of history,” and the calendar making it easier to track how many days or years pass before “spawn becomes a hellzone again” and to record things like war length and horse birth/death for tombstones—**“Basically 2b2t without hacks. Pseudo-Anarchy.”**
